AI can make code review faster, but it should not replace the reviewer who understands the product, its users, and its constraints.

## Start with narrow questions

Ask for concrete checks: error handling paths, untested edge cases, unclear names, or likely performance problems. Narrow prompts lead to comments you can verify.

## Treat suggestions as hypotheses

An assistant can point at a problem, but it cannot own the decision. Read the surrounding code, reproduce the concern, and decide whether it matters in your system.

<Callout>The best use of AI review is increasing the number of thoughtful questions—not automatically accepting more changes.</Callout>

## Keep the review human

Design, security posture, maintenance cost, and product intent still need context. Use the tool to speed up routine inspection, then spend the saved time on the decisions that deserve care.
